  • 1285 A.D.

    The Genesis

    Osman I becomes the leader, later known as sultan, of the Turkish tribes in Anatolia. The tribe will become known as the Ottoman Turks.

  • 1326 A.D.

    The Capital

    Osman I and his army celebrate after they lay siege to Bursa, a city in northwest Turkey. Osman I makes Bursa the capital of the Ottoman Empire and dies the same year.

  • 1453 A.D.

    Young and Victorious

    The 21-year-old sultan Mehmed II lays siege to Constantinople, the Byzantine empire's capital, and after eight weeks of intense fighting, the Ottoman Empire comes out victorious. Renamed Istanbul, the city becomes the Ottoman Empire's capital and is recognized as a cultural, economic, and political mecca for the Empire. There is also a new and unique tax that is imposed on non-Muslim residents.

  • 1517 A.D.

    Selim v. Amun

    The Ottoman sultan Selim I, known as Selim the Grim, captures Cairo, conquers Egypt, and ends Mameluke rule in the middle east.

  • 1520 A.D.

    A Magnificent

    Sultan Suleyman I, known as Suleyman the Magnificent, rises to power and conquers parts of southeast Europe and western Asia in his conquest to convert people to Islam. The Ottoman empire peaked under Suleyman I rule, and he is remembered as one of the most infamous and powerful sultans.

  • 1571 A.D.

    The Begining of a Long Ending

    Unlike his father, Suleyman I, Selim II is cowardly and disorganized. Under his reign, the Ottomans fail to conquer the Venetian island of Cyprus during the Battle of Lepanto. Spanish and Venetian galleys demolish the Turkish fleet. The slow decline of the Ottoman Empire begins.

  • 1683 A.D.

    A Loss of Superiority

    The Ottomans lead an expedition against emperor Leopold I and try to conquer Vienna. The Ottomans are defeated by the combined force of Leopold I and John III Sobieski of Poland, marking the end of the Turkish superiority in eastern Europe.

  • 1697 A.D.

    Forced to Peace

    Austrian troops push into Ottoman territory in Hungary, defeat the Turks at Zenta, and force the Ottoman empire to give up land in that region to keep the peace.

  • 1774 A.D.

    Meddling in Affairs

    The treaty of Kuchuk Kainarji is signed, and the Ottoman empire secedes Crimea to Russia, ending the Russo-Turkish War. The conclusion of the Ottoman's undisputed control of the Black Sea gives Russia a diplomatic basis for any future intervention in the Ottoman Empires' internal affairs.

  • 1821 A.D.

    Greece is Back

    Greece begins to rebel against the Ottomans and eventually gets help from Britain, France, and Russia. Greece wins its independence in 1830.

  • 1878 A.D.

    Losing More

    The Congress of Berlin is signed replacing the Treaty of San Stefano, which was signed at the conclusion of the Russo-Turkish War. The treaty acknowledges the independence of Serbia, Romania, Montenegro, and Bulgaria freeing them from the Ottoman Empire.

  • 1914 A.D.

    Fin

    Post-Balkan War, the Ottoman Empire sides with the Central Powers during World War I, and they lose the war. Peace treaties cause the Ottoman Empire to dissolve, and the Republic of Turkey is established in 1923.
